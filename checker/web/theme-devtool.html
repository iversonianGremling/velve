How you spell colors

Brand hue

Vividness

Contrast

Accent saturation per mode — the gap between them is your “vibrancy contrast”. Keep light bold, dark soft (Helmholtz–Kohlrausch + halation make full saturation buzz on dark).

Brand color (hex)

Accepted as-is, converted to OKLCH so it still derives a full ladder. (In velve a raw hex counts as a tweak — the analyzer can nudge you toward a token.)

Paste a palette (hex, any separator)

Sorted by lightness and mapped onto the tone rungs — so an imported brand palette *becomes* your surfaces/text, light & dark.

Pin primary to an exact colour

The button becomes exactly this colour; onPrimary is solved for the most legible text on it (watch the badge — a mid-tone brand colour may not clear AA, which is the honest signal). The other accents derive from its hue; dark mode lifts & softens it.

Accent engine

Seats the button at its hue's most-saturated lightness — lime/amber rise & brighten, blue/violet stay mid. Legibility shown as APCA Lc (≈60 = UI / large text, ≈75 = body). Toggle off to compare.
